# DG Cap Rate Benchmarks -- Reference Data for Appraisals

This document provides market-derived cap rate benchmarks for Dollar General net lease properties. These tables serve as the baseline for Agent-DG's automated BOV (Broker Opinion of Value) engine. All figures reflect institutional-grade single-tenant net lease (STNL) transactions on absolute NNN terms with corporate-guaranteed (investment-grade) leases.

---

## Cap Rate by Remaining Lease Term

The single strongest determinant of DG cap rates is remaining lease term. Buyers pay a premium for duration because it delays re-leasing risk. Every year of term lost widens the cap rate.

| Remaining Term | Cap Rate Range | Typical Buyer Profile |
|----------------|---------------|----------------------|
| 15 years (new) | 6.50% - 6.90% | 1031 exchange, institutional, REIT feeder |
| 12-14 years | 6.70% - 7.10% | 1031 exchange, private equity |
| 10 years | 6.90% - 7.20% | Experienced NNN investors, family offices |
| 7 years | 7.25% - 7.75% | Value-add buyers, regional investors |
| 5 years | 7.75% - 8.25% | Opportunistic, re-lease-capable buyers |
| 3 years or less | 8.50%+ | Local operators, developers banking on renewal |

**Benchmark midpoints used by Agent-DG:**
- 15yr new: **6.70%**
- 10yr: **7.00%**
- 7yr: **7.50%**
- 5yr: **8.00%**
- 3yr: **8.50%+**

**Key dynamics:**
- Each year of lease term below 15 adds approximately 10-15 bps to cap rate.
- Below 5 years remaining, the curve steepens sharply -- buyers price in vacancy/re-leasing risk.
- Renewal option years are NOT counted as firm term. A 5yr remaining with three 5yr options is still priced as a 5yr deal, though options provide a soft floor.
- Rent bumps (10% every 5 years is standard DG) compress cap rates by 15-25 bps vs. flat-rent leases.

## Deal Volume Trends (2023-2025)

The DG net lease market has cooled from peak 2021-2022 levels. Cap rate expansion reflects broader interest rate normalization.

| Year | Trend | Avg Cap Rate (15yr new) | Volume Notes |
|------|-------|------------------------|-------------|
| 2021 | Peak compression | ~5.75% | Record low caps. 1031 flood. Sub-6% common. |
| 2022 | Late-cycle peak | ~6.00% | Rate hikes begin mid-year. Volume still strong H1. |
| 2023 | Expansion begins | ~6.50% | 150+ bps of expansion from trough. Volume down 30-40%. Bid-ask gap wide. |
| 2024 | Stabilization | ~6.65% | Spreads narrow. Selective buyer re-engagement. Volume recovering but below 2021-22 levels. |
| 2025 (YTD) | Cautious equilibrium | ~6.70% | Cap rates flattening. Buyers pricing in rate-plateau. DG credit concerns (S&P downgrade to BBB-) adding 10-20 bps. |

**Credit watch factor:** DG's S&P rating was downgraded from BBB to BBB- in late 2024 amid margin pressure and shrink concerns. This adds 10-20 bps to cap rates vs. the 2021-2022 era when DG traded at a premium to other dollar store credits. Agent-DG incorporates this adjustment automatically.

## How Agent-DG Uses These Benchmarks

1. **Auto-BOV generation:** When a DG property is identified (via DOR scrape or manual entry), Agent-DG pulls remaining lease term, state, and construction vintage to select the appropriate cap rate from these tables.
2. **Indicated value:** `Indicated Value = Annual Rent (NOI) / Cap Rate`. Agent-DG calculates NOI from the lease abstract and divides by the benchmark cap rate.
3. **Comp validation:** The auto-generated BOV is cross-checked against the 5 most recent closed comps in the same state/region (from the 05-comps database).
4. **Adjustment grid:** Agent-DG applies adjustments for: lot size vs. avg, traffic count, co-tenancy, corner vs. interior, rent-to-sales ratio, and DG Market/DGX format premium/discount.
5. **Output:** BOV is embedded in the OM template and the "I Got A Buyer" letter as `{{INDICATED_VALUE}}` and `{{CAP_RATE}}`.
