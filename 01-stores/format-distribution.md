# DG Format Distribution -- National Mix

Dollar General operates under multiple store formats, each with different square footage, merchandise mix, target demographics, and net lease investment profiles. This document catalogs the full format portfolio and its implications for STNL brokerage.

---

## Format Summary

| Format | Est. Count | % of Portfolio | Sq Ft Range | Launch Year | Target Market |
|--------|-----------|---------------|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Traditional DG** | 20,000+ | ~93% | 7,500 - 9,100 sqft | 1939 | Rural and suburban. The fortress. |
| **DG Market** | 200+ | ~1% | 12,000 - 16,000 sqft | 2023 | Food deserts. Full grocery component. |
| **DGX** | 100+ | <1% | 3,600 sqft | 2017 | Urban metros. Convenience-forward. |
| **pOpshelf** | 200+ | ~1% | 9,000 sqft | 2020 | Suburban. $5-and-under non-consumables. |
| **Mi Super Dollar General** | 1+ | <0.01% | ~9,000 sqft | 2023 | Mexico. Testing phase. |

**Total fleet:** 20,000+ locations across 48 states as of FY2025 (DG does not operate in Alaska or Hawaii). Dollar General is the largest retailer by store count in the United States.

## Traditional Dollar General

The backbone. 93% of the portfolio. The most liquid single-tenant net lease asset in the country.

### Specifications

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Building size** | 7,500 sqft (legacy) to 9,100 sqft (current prototype). Some older stores as small as 6,800 sqft. |
| **Lot size** | 0.75 - 1.5 acres typical. Freestanding with dedicated parking (25-40 spaces). |
| **Construction** | Steel frame, concrete block, or wood frame. Metal roof (standing seam or R-panel). |
| **Lease structure** | 15 years initial term, three 5-year renewal options. NNN (tenant pays taxes, insurance, CAM). |
| **Rent bumps** | 10% increase every 5 years (standard). Some legacy leases have flat rent or 7.5% bumps. |
| **Typical rent** | $85,000 - $130,000/year ($10 - $14/sqft). Varies by market and vintage. |
| **Landlord responsibility** | Roof and structure only (per most leases). Practically zero during initial term. |
| **Target demographics** | Households earning $40,000 or less. Rural communities, small towns, exurban corridors. |
| **Typical location** | State highway frontage, small-town main street, county road intersection. 3-5 mile trade area. |

### Investment Profile

| Metric | Range |
|--------|-------|
| Purchase price | $1.0M - $2.3M |
| Cap rate (15yr new) | 6.50% - 6.90% |
| Cap rate (10yr remaining) | 7.00% - 7.25% |
| Price per sqft | $130 - $250 |
| Buyer pool depth | **Deepest of any STNL asset**. 1031 exchangers, REITs, private equity, family offices, self-directed IRA buyers. |

### Why Traditional DG is the Most Liquid STNL Asset

1. **Investment-grade tenant.** Dollar General Corporation (NYSE: DG). BBB- rated (S&P). $38B+ revenue.
2. **Recession-resistant.** Dollar stores outperform in downturns -- customers trade down. DG same-store sales grew during 2008-2009 and 2020.
3. **Low price point.** $1M-$2M fits the 1031 exchange sweet spot. Accessible to individual investors without institutional fund requirements.
4. **Absolute NNN.** Zero landlord management. The definition of passive income.
5. **Standardized lease.** Every DG lease is essentially identical. Buyers know exactly what they are getting. Due diligence is fast.
6. **National footprint.** 20,000+ stores = 20,000+ potential listings. Deep comp database.

## DGX (Dollar General Express)

Urban convenience format launched in Nashville in 2017. Designed for dense metro neighborhoods where a traditional DG footprint is too large or land cost is prohibitive.

### Specifications

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Building size** | 3,600 sqft |
| **Location** | Urban cores, pedestrian-traffic corridors, college towns |
| **Merchandise** | Snacks, beverages, health/beauty, grab-and-go. Curated SKU count (~3,500 vs. ~11,000 traditional). |
| **Markets** | Nashville (original), expanding to select metros |
| **Lease structure** | Varies. Some in multi-tenant buildings (not STNL). Freestanding DGX are rare. |

### STNL Implications

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Liquidity | **Low.** Fewer than 100 freestanding locations. Buyers are unfamiliar with the format. |
| Comp availability | **Very limited.** Insufficient transaction data for reliable cap rate benchmarks. |
| Lease terms | **Non-standard.** May differ from traditional DG corporate lease. Verify individually. |
| Buyer pool | **Narrow.** Urban NNN buyers exist but typically seek Starbucks, Chipotle, or medical tenants in this size range. |
| Agent-DG treatment | **Flag only.** DGX properties are flagged in the pipeline but not prioritized for automated BOV or direct mail. |

## DG Market

Food-forward large-format store targeting USDA-designated food deserts. Full produce, meat, and dairy departments alongside traditional DG consumables.

### Specifications

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Building size** | 12,000 - 16,000 sqft |
| **Lot size** | 1.5 - 2.5 acres |
| **Merchandise** | Full grocery (produce, meat, dairy, frozen) + traditional DG assortment |
| **Target** | Rural communities without grocery access within 10+ miles |
| **Lease structure** | 15 years, NNN. Standard DG corporate guarantee. Bumps align with traditional format. |
| **Typical rent** | $140,000 - $200,000/year |

### STNL Implications

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Liquidity | **Moderate.** Larger footprint = different buyer pool. Competes with grocery-anchored retail, not traditional DG. |
| Cap rate | **Slightly tighter** than traditional DG in same market -- grocery component perceived as essential service. Expect 6.40% - 6.70% for new construction. |
| Comp availability | **Limited but growing.** 200+ stores provides a small but expanding dataset. |
| Buyer pool | Institutional buyers comfortable with grocery footprints. Less appeal to typical 1031 DG buyer due to higher price point ($2M - $3.5M). |
| Re-lease risk | **Lower than traditional DG.** Larger building with grocery infrastructure could attract regional grocery operators, dollar stores, or community health tenants. |
| Agent-DG treatment | **Include with format flag.** BOV generated using DG Market-specific cap rate adjustment (-15 bps vs. traditional). |

## pOpshelf

Non-consumable concept targeting suburban households earning $50,000-$125,000. All items priced $5 and under. Positioned as a "treasure hunt" shopping experience -- the TJ Maxx of dollar retail.

### Specifications

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Building size** | ~9,000 sqft |
| **Location** | Suburban strip centers and freestanding. Higher-traffic, higher-income trade areas than traditional DG. |
| **Merchandise** | Home decor, health/beauty, party supplies, crafts, seasonal. No grocery. |
| **Store count** | 200+ standalone locations across 21 states |
| **Shop-in-shop** | DG tested pOpshelf sections inside traditional DG stores. **Discontinued** in 2024 due to cannibalization of DG's core consumable business. |

### STNL Implications

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| Liquidity | **Low.** Unproven concept. No established track record of lease renewals. Buyers price in concept risk. |
| Cap rate | **Wider than traditional DG.** Expect 7.50% - 8.50%+ even on new construction. Concept risk premium of 75-150 bps. |
| Lease guarantee | **Verify carefully.** Some pOpshelf leases are guaranteed by DG Corp (investment-grade). Others may be guaranteed by a subsidiary. The guarantee is everything. |
| Buyer pool | **Narrow.** Specialty retail NNN buyers. Not the typical DG investor. |
| Agent-DG treatment | **Exclude from automated campaigns.** pOpshelf properties are tracked but not included in BOV generation or direct mail. Too much concept risk for a stock template. |

### pOpshelf Strategic Uncertainty

DG has sent mixed signals on pOpshelf's future. Shop-in-shop was abandoned. Standalone store growth has decelerated. CEO commentary in Q3 FY2025 earnings call described pOpshelf as "under strategic review." For STNL purposes, treat pOpshelf as a speculative asset until DG provides clarity on long-term commitment.

## Growth Trajectory (FY2025 Guidance)

Dollar General is shifting from raw unit growth to portfolio optimization. New store openings are decelerating while remodels and relocations accelerate.

| Activity | FY2025 Plan | FY2024 Actual | FY2023 Actual | Trend |
|----------|-------------|---------------|---------------|-------|
| **New stores** | 575 | 730 | 987 | Decelerating. Down 42% from FY2023 peak. |
| **Remodels** | 4,300 | 1,500 | 1,000 | **Accelerating sharply.** 3x increase. Priority is refreshing the existing fleet. |
| **Relocations** | 45 | 85 | 110 | Slowing. Most strategic relocations completed. |
| **Closures** | Not disclosed | ~50 est. | ~45 est. | Minimal. DG rarely closes stores -- occupancy cost is too low. |

### What This Means for NNN Brokers

1. **Fewer new construction listings entering the market.** 575 new stores in FY2025 vs. 987 in FY2023. The supply of 15yr new DG assets is shrinking. Scarcity compresses cap rates on new deals.
2. **Remodels extend the usable life of existing stores.** A remodeled store signals DG's commitment to the location. Buyers may pay a premium for recently remodeled DG properties.
3. **The action is in the existing portfolio.** With 20,000+ stores and decelerating new builds, the opportunity for brokers is listing existing stores (5-12 years remaining term) from aging landlords, estate sales, and portfolio dispositions. This is exactly what Agent-DG is built to find.
4. **Format optimization creates niche opportunities.** DG Market conversions (traditional to DG Market) may generate lease amendments or new leases. Track these for re-listing opportunities.
