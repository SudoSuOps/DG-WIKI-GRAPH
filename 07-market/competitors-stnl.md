# STNL Competitor Landscape — DG vs the Field

## Overview

Dollar General competes for net lease investor capital against a defined set of single-tenant retail credits. This document profiles each major competitor and evaluates DG's positioning in the STNL marketplace.

## Competitor Profiles

### Dollar Tree / Family Dollar

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Credit Rating | BBB- (S&P) |
| Unit Count | 16,300+ combined (8,100 DT / 8,200 FD) |
| Typical Lease Term | 10-15 years NNN |
| Cap Rate Range | 4.82% - 7.18% |
| Avg Building Size | 8,000-12,000 SF (DT), 8,000-10,000 SF (FD) |
| Rent PSF | $8.00 - $14.00 |

**Key dynamics:**
- Wide cap rate spread reflects quality disparity — new DT stores at the low end, older FD at the high end
- Combo stores (Dollar Tree + Family Dollar under one roof) gaining traction with investors, 12,000-14,000 SF
- Family Dollar brand underperforming — 600+ closures announced in 2024
- Dollar Tree exploring strategic alternatives for the FD segment
- **Investor takeaway**: DT-branded stores trade tighter; FD-branded stores carry brand risk premium

### AutoZone (AZO)

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Credit Rating | BBB (S&P) |
| Unit Count | 7,100+ domestic |
| Typical Lease Term | 15-20 years NNN |
| Avg Cap Rate | 5.34% |
| Avg Building Size | 3,500-4,500 SF (hub: 7,000+ SF) |
| Rent PSF | $18.00 - $28.00 |

**Key dynamics:**
- Best-performing auto parts retailer — insulated from e-commerce (customers need parts NOW)
- Consistent 15%+ annual EPS growth over the last decade
- Aggressive share buyback program — shares outstanding down 75% since 2012
- Smaller footprint = lower total rent basis = lower absolute deal size ($800K-$1.5M)
- **Investor takeaway**: Tightest caps in the auto parts category, premium justified by execution

### Tractor Supply Company (TSCO)

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Credit Rating | BBB (S&P) |
| Unit Count | 2,200+ |
| Typical Lease Term | 10-15 years NNN |
| Cap Rate Range | 5.50% - 7.00% |

**Key dynamics:**
- Largest rural lifestyle retailer — **direct geographic overlap with Dollar General**
- Same customer demographic: rural, value-conscious, essential goods
- Larger footprint = higher absolute deal size ($2M-$4M)
- Strong same-store sales performance through 2024
- Pet and animal feed categories drive traffic and repeat visits
- **Investor takeaway**: Direct DG market comp — same towns, same demographics, tighter caps

## Why DG Competes Favorably

1. **Essential goods retailer** — 80%+ of revenue from consumables (food, cleaning, health/beauty)
2. **20,000-unit base** — largest physical footprint in net lease retail, deep liquidity
3. **Rural dominance** — 75% of stores in towns under 20,000 population, limited competition
4. **Predictable rent bumps** — 10% every 5 years, contractual escalation
5. **Low rent basis** — $85,000-$100,000 annual rent on a $250K build cost, 2.5-3.0% rent-to-sales
6. **Corporate guarantee** — 100% company-operated, no franchise risk
7. **BBB credit** — investment-grade, same rating as AutoZone and Tractor Supply

## Why DG Faces Headwinds

1. **Stock decline** — DG shares down 60% from 2022 highs ($262 to ~$105 in late 2024)
2. **Profit erosion** — operating profit down 49.2% YoY in FY2024
3. **Same-store sales weakness** — only +1.2% SSS growth, below CPI inflation
4. **pOpshelf failure** — strategic small-format initiative abandoned, 99 stores closed, $72M write-down
5. **Shrinkage crisis** — $0.5B+ in incremental inventory shrink, driving margin compression
6. **OSHA scrutiny** — repeated safety violations, blocked aisles, fines exceeding $21M cumulative
7. **CEO transition** — Todd Vasos returned from retirement in 2023 to stabilize; market skeptical on turnaround timeline
8. **Supply oversaturation** — 20,000 units = enormous volume of product available for investors, compresses demand per unit

## Broker Takeaway

DG remains the **most liquid STNL retail product** in the market — nothing else offers 20,000 units of investment-grade corporate-guaranteed net lease with built-in escalations. But the credit story has deteriorated since 2022, and pricing reflects it. The best comp for DG valuation today is Tractor Supply — same credit, same geography, same customer — and TSC trades 50-100 bps tighter because the operational story is cleaner.

For buyers: DG at 6.75% is a yield play with a margin of safety if the turnaround holds. For sellers: the window to exit at sub-6.50% caps on older assets has closed.
