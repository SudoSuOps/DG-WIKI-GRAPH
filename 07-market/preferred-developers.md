# DG Preferred Developers — Who Builds Dollar Generals

**Source**: Developer websites, DG corporate disclosures, CoStar construction pipeline, industry interviews
**Relevance**: Build-to-suit developers are the source of the freshest DG inventory. New construction = day-one 15-year lease = highest value asset. Know who builds them.

## The Developer Model

Dollar General does NOT build its own stores. DG uses a **preferred developer network** — regional builders with long-term relationships who deliver pad-ready, build-to-suit locations to DG's exact specifications.

```
THE PIPELINE:
  1. DG real estate team identifies a market
  2. DG selects a preferred developer for that region
  3. Developer acquires the land (1.0 - 1.5 acres)
  4. Developer builds to DG spec (9,100 sqft, 30 parking spaces)
  5. DG signs a 15-year NNN lease at agreed rent
  6. Certificate of Occupancy issued → store opens
  7. Developer sells the completed asset to a net lease investor
  
  THE KEY: Step 7 is where YOU come in.
  The developer is almost always a SELLER — they build to sell.
  They do not hold long-term. Their business is TURNOVER.
```

## The Build-to-Suit Economics

```
DEVELOPER COST STRUCTURE (typical new DG):
  Land acquisition:         $75,000 - $250,000 (varies wildly by market)
  Site work:                $150,000 - $300,000 (grading, utilities, parking)
  Building construction:    $550,000 - $750,000 (steel frame, 9,100 sqft)
  Soft costs:               $75,000 - $125,000 (permits, engineering, legal)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Total development cost:   $850,000 - $1,425,000
  
SALE PRICE (new 15-year NNN):
  At 6.50% cap, $110K rent:  $1,692,308
  At 6.75% cap, $110K rent:  $1,629,630
  At 6.85% cap, $110K rent:  $1,605,839
  
DEVELOPER SPREAD:
  Cost: ~$1.1M (mid-range) → Sale: ~$1.65M → Profit: ~$550K (50% margin)
  
  THIS IS WHY DEVELOPERS BUILD DGs:
  Predictable spec + guaranteed tenant + immediate sale = repeatable profit.
  They don't hold. They flip. Your job is to be the broker on the flip.
```

## Why This Matters for STNL Brokers

```
1. FRESHEST INVENTORY
   Build-to-suit = brand new building + day-one 15-year lease.
   This is the highest-value DG asset in the market.
   All 8 rent bumps intact. All 6 options ahead. Zero deferred maintenance.

2. DEVELOPER IS A MOTIVATED SELLER
   Developers build to SELL. They need capital recycling to fund the next build.
   They are not emotional owners — they price to move.
   Typical hold after CO: 30-90 days. They want your buyer list.

3. PIPELINE VISIBILITY
   Construction permits are public record. If you monitor DG permits in
   your market, you can identify deals 6-12 months before they hit the market.
   
   SOURCES:
     - County building permit databases
     - Dodge Data & Analytics (construction starts)
     - CoStar construction pipeline
     - DG 10-Q/10-K (new store openings by quarter)
     - Developer websites (some list active projects)

4. RELATIONSHIP LEVERAGE
   If you sell a developer's first DG, you sell their next 20.
   Developers build 5-15 DGs per year. One relationship = recurring inventory.
   This is the definition of a repeatable book of business.

5. EARLY DEAL SIGNALS
   Track these in your market:
     - Land sales to known DG developers (1.0-1.5 acre parcels)
     - Rezoning applications for retail on rural highway parcels
     - DG building permit applications (9,100 sqft retail, specific)
     - Utility connection requests for new retail construction
```
