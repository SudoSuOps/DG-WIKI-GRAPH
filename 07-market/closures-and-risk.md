# DG Store Closures & Dark Store Risk

**Updated**: 2026-04-07
**Sources**: DG 10-K FY2025, RetailWire, Fast Company, FinanceBuzz

## Closure Numbers

```
FY2024 (Jan 2024):
  Closed: 27 stores
  Opened: 72 stores
  Net: +45 stores (growth year)

FY2025 (announced March 2025):
  Closing: 96 Dollar General stores (Q1 FY2025)
  Closing: 45 pOpshelf stores
  Converting: 6 pOpshelf → Dollar General
  TOTAL: 141 closures across both brands

CONTEXT:
  20,959 total stores
  141 closures = 0.67% of store base
  "Less than one percent" — CEO Todd Vasos

  This is NOT a mass closure. This is pruning.
  96 DG closures out of 20,959 = portfolio optimization.
```

## What Closures Mean for STNL Investors

```
FOR OWNERS OF CLOSING STORES:
  DG vacates → property goes dark
  Dark store = no tenant, no NNN income
  Value drops: from $1.5M (leased) to $400-600K (vacant)
  Owner must: re-tenant, sell at discount, or redevelop
  
  THIS IS THE RISK every DG buyer must understand.
  Less than 1% per year — but if it's YOUR store, it's 100%.

FOR BUYERS IN THE MARKET:
  96 closures = 96 MOTIVATED SELLERS
  Dark or soon-to-be-dark properties priced at discount
  Opportunity for: re-tenant with another dollar store, 
                    convert to auto parts, medical office
  Cap rates on at-risk properties: 8.50%+ (steep discount)
  
FOR AGENT-DG:
  Monitor closure announcements → contact affected owners IMMEDIATELY
  "I know your DG is closing. Here's what your property is worth today.
   Here's a buyer who wants to re-tenant or redevelop."
  
  Closures are DEAL FLOW for the prepared broker.
```

## Dark Store Risk Factors

| Risk Signal | What It Means | Cap Rate Impact |
|-------------|--------------|----------------|
| Lease < 3 years remaining | DG may not renew | +100-150 bps |
| No remodel in last 5 years | DG not investing in the location | +50-75 bps |
| Population declining in area | Customer base shrinking | +25-50 bps |
| New DG opened within 3 miles | Cannibalization risk | +25-50 bps |
| NN lease (not NNN) | Older store, landlord has roof liability | +50-75 bps |
| pOpshelf co-tenant | pOpshelf strategy uncertain | +25-50 bps |

## The Remodel Signal

```
DG REMODEL = COMMITMENT TO RENEW

  4,300 remodels planned FY2025 (20% of store fleet)
  A remodel costs DG $150K-$300K per store
  DG does NOT invest $200K in a store they plan to leave
  
  IF YOUR DG WAS RECENTLY REMODELED:
    → DG is committed to the location for at least one more option period
    → Renewal probability: 85%+ (vs 60% for non-remodeled)
    → Cap rate: 25-50 bps tighter than non-remodeled
    
  Agent-DG checks: "Has this store been remodeled in the last 3 years?"
  If YES → stronger listing pitch to the owner
  If NO → risk factor for the buyer
```

## Mom & Pop Investor Considerations

The typical individual DG investor is NOT a sophisticated institutional buyer. They're often:

```
MOM & POP DG BUYER PROFILE:
  - California couple selling a rental property ($1-3M equity)
  - Retiring business owner doing a 1031 exchange
  - Doctor/dentist seeking passive income
  - Small business owner diversifying into real estate
  - Inheritor managing a family portfolio
  
  WHAT THEY DON'T KNOW:
  ✗ Many DGs are in towns with population 228 or 881
  ✗ Rural locations may be hard to re-tenant if DG leaves
  ✗ NN leases put roof/structure on the landlord
  ✗ DG stock dropped 60% in 2024 (does NOT affect the lease, but scares them)
  ✗ Not all DGs are the same — new NNN ≠ old NN

```
