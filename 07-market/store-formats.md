# DG Store Formats — From Rural 9,100 sqft to Urban 3,600 sqft

**Source**: DG 10-K (FY2026), investor presentations, press releases, CoStar, industry reporting
**Relevance**: Format determines lease size, location type, cap rate, and buyer profile. Know what you're selling.

## Format Overview

Dollar General operates five distinct store formats. Each serves a different market, carries a different footprint, and trades at a different cap rate.

| Format | Size (sqft) | Market | Locations | Launched | Status |
|--------|------------|--------|-----------|----------|--------|
| Traditional DG | 7,500 - 9,100 | Rural / small town | 20,000+ | 1939 | Core — 95%+ of fleet |
| DGX | 3,600 | Urban / metro | Select markets | 2017 (Nashville) | Growing — targeted expansion |
| DG Market | 16,000 | Food desert communities | Limited | 2021 | Pilot — select locations |
| pOpshelf | 9,000 | Suburban | 200+ across 21 states | 2020 | Under review — 45 closures FY26 |
| Mi Super Dollar General | Varies | Mexico | Escobedo, Nuevo Leon | March 2023 | International pilot |

## Traditional Dollar General — The Core Asset

```
THE WORKHORSE:
  Size:               7,500 - 9,100 sq ft (standard is 9,100 new build)
  Lot:                1.0 - 1.5 acres
  Parking:            30 spaces
  Location:           Rural, small town, secondary highway
  Population:         Towns under 20,000 (75-80% of fleet)
  Customer:           Household income $30K-$50K
  SKUs:               ~11,500
  Categories:         Consumables (80%), seasonal, home products, apparel
  
  STNL PROFILE:
    Lease term:       15 years NNN (new construction)
    Cap rate:         6.50% - 6.85% (new), 7.50-8.50% (existing)
    Price range:      $1.2M - $2.0M
    Buyer:            1031 exchange, HNW individual, DST, REIT
    Liquidity:        HIGHEST — most actively traded net lease asset
```

This is 95%+ of what you'll sell. The 9,100 sqft rural DG is the default net lease comp.

## DGX — The Urban Play

```
THE CONVENIENCE FORMAT:
  Size:               3,600 sq ft (60% smaller than traditional)
  Location:           Urban core, metro neighborhoods, college towns
  First store:        Nashville, TN (2017)
  Population:         Dense urban — 50,000+ metro
  Customer:           Urban dweller, convenience-driven, younger demographic
  
  DIFFERENTIATORS:
    - Smaller footprint fits urban infill parcels
    - Select locations carry fresh produce and coffee
    - Higher rent per sqft ($18-25/sqft vs $12-16 for traditional)
    - Competes with convenience stores, not Walmart
    - Curated SKU assortment — higher-margin mix
  
  STNL PROFILE:
    Lease term:       15 years NNN
    Annual rent:      $65,000 - $90,000 (lower total, higher per sqft)
    Cap rate:         6.25% - 6.75% (tighter — urban land premium)
    Price range:      $960K - $1.4M
    Buyer:            Urban infill investors, REIT urban allocation
    Liquidity:        MODERATE — fewer comps, niche buyer pool
    
  WATCH: Smaller rent = smaller deal = lower commission. But urban
  locations often appreciate faster. Know your buyer.
```

## DG Market — The Grocery Format

```
THE FOOD DESERT SOLUTION:
  Size:               16,000 sq ft (nearly 2x traditional)
  Location:           Underserved communities lacking full-service grocery
  Customer:           Food desert residents — limited alternative options
  Categories:         Expanded grocery, fresh produce, frozen, dairy
  
  DIFFERENTIATORS:
    - Full produce section, cooler/freezer expansion
    - Addresses USDA food desert designation
    - Community anchor — may qualify for government incentives
    - Larger footprint = higher rent, but different buyer profile
  
  STNL PROFILE:
    Lease term:       15 years NNN
    Annual rent:      $150,000 - $200,000+
    Cap rate:         6.50% - 7.00%
    Price range:      $2.1M - $3.1M
    Buyer:            Institutional, impact investors, larger 1031
    Liquidity:        LOW — limited inventory, few comps
    
  NOTE: Very few DG Markets exist. If you encounter one, it's a
  specialty listing. Price it carefully — the comp pool is thin.
```

## pOpshelf — The Suburban Experiment

```
THE $5-AND-UNDER FORMAT:
  Size:               9,000 sq ft
  Locations:          200+ across 21 states
  Target demo:        Suburban women, mid-40s, HHI $50K-$125K
  Price points:       70% of items $5 or less
  Categories:         Home decor, beauty, party, seasonal, crafts, toys
  Competitors:        Five Below, HomeGoods, TJ Maxx
  
  DIFFERENTIATORS:
    - Treasure hunt format (not consumables-driven)
    - Higher-margin merchandise vs traditional DG
    - Different customer — suburban, higher income
    - Instagram-friendly store design
  
  STNL PROFILE:
    Lease term:       10-15 years NNN
    Guarantor:        Dollar General Corporation (same BBB credit)
    Cap rate:         7.00% - 7.75% (wider — concept risk)
    Buyer:            Investors comfortable with newer concepts
    Liquidity:        LOW — buyers discount unproven formats
  
  CAUTION: DG closed 45 pOpshelf locations in FY2026. The format is
  under strategic review. Buyers will demand a premium (wider cap)
  to compensate for concept risk. Disclose the closures.
```

```
THE MEXICO PILOT:

  US lease valuations. Monitor quarterly earnings for Mexico updates.
```

## Why Format Matters for STNL Brokers

```
FORMAT → FOOTPRINT → LOCATION → CAP RATE → BUYER POOL

Traditional 9,100 sqft:
  Rural → 6.50-6.85% cap → 1031, DST, HNW, REIT → DEEP buyer pool
  
DGX 3,600 sqft:
  Urban → 6.25-6.75% cap → Urban specialists, REIT → NARROW buyer pool
  
DG Market 16,000 sqft:
  Food desert → 6.50-7.00% cap → Institutional, impact → VERY NARROW
  
pOpshelf 9,000 sqft:
  Suburban → 7.00-7.75% cap → Risk-tolerant investors → THIN market

RULE: Traditional DG is your bread and butter. Everything else is a
specialty listing that requires format-specific knowledge and a
targeted buyer. Don't price a pOpshelf like a traditional DG.
```

## Format Identification Checklist

When you encounter a DG property, confirm the format before pricing:

| Check | How | Why It Matters |
|-------|-----|---------------|
| Square footage | Lease abstract or tax roll | Determines format classification |
| Store signage | Drive-by or Google Street View | DGX and pOpshelf have distinct branding |
| Product mix | Walk the store or check DG locator | Fresh produce = DGX or DG Market |
| Location type | Demographics, population density | Rural = traditional, urban = DGX |
| Lease guarantor | Read the lease | Should ALWAYS be DG Corp, not a franchisee |
