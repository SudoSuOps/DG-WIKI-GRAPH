# Who Buys Dollar General Properties — Buyer Universe

## Overview

Dollar General net lease properties attract a defined buyer universe ranging from individual 1031 exchangers to institutional REITs. Understanding who is on the other side of your deal — and what they care about — is how you price, market, and close.

## Buyer Segments

### 1. 1031 Exchange Investors

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Share of DG deal flow | 30-35% |
| Typical buyer profile | Individual, sold apartment/commercial, passive income goal |
| Avg deal size | $1.0M - $2.5M |
| Time pressure | 45-day identification, 180-day close (IRC Section 1031) |
| Key motivation | Tax deferral, passive NNN income, estate planning |

**Behavioral notes:**
- Will pay 10-25 bps tighter cap rate under deadline pressure
- Prioritize lease term (12+ years remaining) over location quality
- Often first-time NNN buyers — education-intensive, longer diligence
- Strong preference for corporate guarantee over franchise
- Geographic flexibility — willing to buy out-of-state for the right lease

```
TYPICAL 1031 SCENARIO:
Sold: 12-unit apartment building in CA for $3.2M (basis: $1.8M, gain: $1.4M)
Tax at risk: ~$500K (federal + state capital gains)
Target: 2 DG properties at $1.5M each, 6.75% cap
Result: $202,500 annual NNN income, $0 current tax liability
```

### 2. DST (Delaware Statutory Trust) Investors

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Share of DG deal flow | 10-15% (growing) |
| Typical buyer profile | Passive, accredited, older demographics (55+) |
| Minimum investment | $100,000 - $250,000 |
| Key motivation | 1031 exchange into passive fractional ownership |
| Exit options | UPREIT / 721 exchange into operating partnership units |

**Behavioral notes:**
- Cannot make management decisions — truly passive by SEC structure
- DG is a top-3 DST tenant alongside Walgreens and FedEx
- DST sponsors (Inland, AEI, Passco) buy DG properties in bulk at market caps, resell interests at tighter implied yields
- Growing segment as baby boomers exit active real estate ownership
- **UPREIT/721 exchange**: convert DST interest into REIT OP units, defer taxes again

### 3. Institutional Investors

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| REIT allocation to net lease | 25-30% of total REIT AUM |
| Pension fund REIT exposure | 70%+ incorporate REITs in alternatives allocation |
| Typical deal size | $5M+ (portfolio preferred) |
| Key motivation | Predictable cash flow, inflation hedge, diversification |
| Decision makers | Investment committee, 60-120 day approval cycle |

**Key institutional buyers of DG properties:**

| Buyer | Type | Estimated DG Holdings |
|-------|------|----------------------|
| Realty Income (O) | Net lease REIT | 800+ properties |
| Spirit Realty (now merged into Realty Income) | Net lease REIT | 400+ properties |

**Behavioral notes:**
- Buy portfolios at 10-25 bps tighter than one-off market pricing
- Will pay premium for 15-year new construction in growth markets
- Disciplined underwriting — will walk from marginal locations
- Sale-leaseback relationships directly with DG corporate are most prized

### 4. High Net Worth (HNW) Individuals

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Typical net worth | $3M - $25M+ |
| Portfolio size | 3-10 NNN properties |
| Avg deal size | $1.0M - $3.0M per asset |
| Key motivation | Cash flow, wealth preservation, generational transfer |
| Decision speed | 2-4 weeks (fastest buyer segment) |

**Behavioral notes:**
- Repeat buyers — once they own one DG, they typically buy more
- Value simplicity: NNN = no landlord responsibilities, predictable income
- Often use 60-70% LTV debt ($1.5M property, $1M loan, $500K equity)
- At current rates (6.5-7.0% mortgage), leverage is neutral to slightly negative on older assets
- Strong preference for new construction in their home state

### 5. Syndications

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Structure | Pooled capital, LLC or LP |
| Investor count | 50-200 investors per fund |
| Minimum investment | $10,000 - $50,000 |
| Hold period | 5-7 years |
| Target IRR | 8-12% (levered) |

**Behavioral notes:**
- Buy portfolios of 5-20 DG properties to achieve scale
- Sponsor earns acquisition fee (1-2%), asset management fee (1-2%), and promote (20%+ above preferred return)
- Investors receive quarterly distributions and K-1 tax reporting
- Cap rate arbitrage: buy at 7.00%+ in secondary markets, aggregate, refinance, hold for escalation
- Increased activity in 2024-2025 as cap rate expansion creates spread to debt cost

### 6. Net Lease REIT Platforms

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Examples | Realty Income, NNN REIT, Agree Realty, Essential Properties |
| Liquidity | Daily (NYSE listed) |
| Cost of capital | 4.5-5.5% implied cap rate on equity |
| Acquisition volume | $2B-$9B annually (Realty Income alone: $9B in 2022) |
| Key advantage | Accretive spread between cost of capital and acquisition cap rate |

**Behavioral notes:**
- Realty Income's implied cap rate of ~5.0% means a DG acquisition at 6.75% is 175 bps accretive
- Prefer sale-leaseback and portfolio transactions over one-off brokered deals
- Will pass on deals with <10 years remaining term
- Credit committee approval required — standardized underwriting, no exceptions
- Disposition pipeline: REITs regularly sell older DG assets with short remaining term

## 2024-2025 Market Dynamics

### Cap Rate Expansion = Buying Opportunity

```
2021 DG new construction:  5.25% cap  →  $1,752,381 value (on $92K NOI)
2024 DG new construction:  6.75% cap  →  $1,362,963 value (on $92K NOI)

Value decline:  -$389,418 (-22.2%) on the SAME lease, SAME tenant, SAME NOI
```

This repricing has shifted the buyer mix:

- **1031 exchangers**: Still active, but more selective on location
- **Value investors / HNW**: Increasing activity — best yields in a decade
- **Syndications**: Aggressively acquiring at 7.00%+ caps in secondary markets
- **REITs**: Selective — focusing on new construction and sale-leasebacks
- **DST sponsors**: Steady demand from aging boomer demographic

## Broker Takeaway

Know your buyer before you price the deal. A 1031 exchanger on day 35 of identification will pay 6.50%. A syndication buying 10 assets at once will grind you to 7.25%. A REIT with a $5B annual target will pay 6.75% and close in 30 days — if the lease is clean and the term is long. Price to the buyer, not to the market.
