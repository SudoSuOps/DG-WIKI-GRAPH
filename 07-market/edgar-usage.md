# EDGAR — Where It Fits in the DG Graph

EDGAR is the top of the ownership chain, not the bottom. It connects parcel-level owners UP to public parents, not DOWN to store-level contacts.

## What EDGAR Gets You

### 1. Parent-Company Identity and Filings
Dollar General Corporation 10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, proxy filings. Official public-company record, risk disclosures, operating footprint, legal entity references, exhibit lists.

### 2. Subsidiary Lists
DG's 2024 10-K includes "List of Subsidiaries of Dollar General Corporation" in exhibit index. Some owned real estate or guarantor structures sit in subsidiaries, not the top parent. This matters for entity resolution.

### 3. Headquarters / Corporate Mailing Context
The 10-K gives corporate context as a parent-entity anchor. Good for connecting store/parcel ownership back up to the public parent. Does NOT tell you who owns a specific Florida parcel.

### 4. Debt / Guarantor / Structure Clues
Filings reference subsidiaries and guarantors in debt documents. Helps understand which legal entities matter financially when mapping ownership above the store level.

## The Critical Distinction

DG doesn't own most of their stores. They LEASE from third-party landlords.

```
THE OWNERSHIP CHAIN (typical DG STNL):

  PARCEL LAYER (FL DOR / Regrid / ATTOM):
    Parcel at 4521 Highway 19, Perry, FL
    Owner: "DG Southeast Holdings LLC"
    Mailing: PO Box 12345, Orlando, FL 32801

    Parent: Spirit Realty Capital Inc (NYSE: SRC)

    → Portfolio strategy in annual proxy
    → THIS tells you the owner's strategy and scale.

  TENANT LAYER (EDGAR — Dollar General Corp):
    DG Corp (NYSE: DG) — the TENANT, not the owner
    → 10-K: 19,000+ stores, 80% leased
    → Subsidiary list: DG Retail LLC, DG Markets LLC
    → Credit rating: BBB (S&P) — investment grade
    → THIS tells you the LEASE is safe, not who owns the building.
```

## When EDGAR Matters in the MAGIC Flow

```
M — MEETINGS:    EDGAR not needed (owner contact comes from tax roll + Sunbiz)
A — APPRAISALS:  EDGAR helps (DG tenant credit rating = lease security = value)
G — GRIND:       EDGAR not needed (outreach goes to parcel owner, not DG Corp)
I — INK:         EDGAR helps (buyer due diligence — who's the tenant, what's the credit)
C — CLOSE:       EDGAR helps (guarantor verification, subsidiary confirmation)
```
