# DG Cap Rate Trends — Pricing Intelligence for Dollar General STNL

## Current Market Snapshot (2024-2025)

Dollar General net lease cap rates are at **10-year highs** — the highest levels since 2013. The era of sub-6% DG deals is over for the foreseeable future.

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| New construction cap rate | 6.70% - 6.75% | Boulder Group Q4 2024 |
| Existing / older vintage | 7.50% - 8.50%+ | Net Lease World |
| Dollar store sector avg (early 2024) | 6.63% | Stan Johnson / SRS |
| Dollar store sector avg (late 2023) | 6.33% | Stan Johnson / SRS |
| YoY increase (early 2024) | +30 bps | Boulder Group |
| New vs existing premium | 75-100 bps | Industry consensus |
| Historical average (2015-2022) | 5.00% - 6.70% | Boulder Group |

### What 30 bps Means on a DG Deal

On a $1.4M DG property at $92,000 NOI:

```
At 6.33% cap:  $1,453,396 value
At 6.63% cap:  $1,387,632 value
At 6.75% cap:  $1,362,963 value
At 7.50% cap:  $1,226,667 value

30 bps of cap rate expansion = ~$65,000 of value erosion on a single asset.
75 bps (new vs old) = ~$90,000 spread.
```

## Cap Rate Compression Factors

These push cap rates DOWN (higher prices):

- **BBB credit rating** — investment-grade corporate guarantee (S&P BBB, Moody's Baa2)
- **New 15-year NNN lease** — maximum remaining term, predictable cash flow
- **Corporate guarantee** — not franchisee, DG is 100% corporate-operated
- **Essential retail classification** — grocery, consumables, pandemic-resistant
- **10% rent bumps** — built-in escalation every 5 years
- **High-traffic location** — pad site, outparcel, or hard-corner with strong ingress/egress
- **1031 exchange demand** — motivated buyers with tight deadlines accept tighter yields

## Cap Rate Expansion Factors

These push cap rates UP (lower prices):

- **Elevated mortgage rates** — 30-year fixed at 6.5-7.0%, debt cost exceeds cap rate on older assets
- **DG stock decline** — down 60% from 2022 highs ($262 to ~$105)
- **Operating profit erosion** — operating profit down 49.2% YoY (FY2024)
- **Same-store sales weakness** — only +1.2% SSS growth, below inflation
- **pOpshelf failure** — strategic initiative abandoned, 99 stores closed
- **Shrinkage and labor costs** — $0.5B+ in incremental shrink expense
- **Short remaining lease term** — below 10 years, cap rates jump 100-200 bps
- **Deferred maintenance** — older prototypes (pre-2015) with roof/HVAC issues

### Why DG Trades Wide of AutoZone / TSC

1. **Volume saturation** — 20,000 units means oversupply of product for buyers
2. **Headline risk** — media coverage of store conditions, labor issues, OSHA fines
3. **Operational headwinds** — margin compression is real, not speculative
4. **Rural concentration** — secondary/tertiary market locations carry higher risk premium

### Why DG Still Trades Tight of Advance Auto

1. **Stronger credit** — BBB vs BBB- (one notch matters in net lease)
2. **Essential goods** — consumables/grocery vs discretionary auto parts
3. **Unit economics** — $250K build cost, low rent basis, hard to replicate
4. **Corporate guarantee** — 100% corporate, zero franchise risk

## Broker Takeaway

The current cap rate environment is the **best buying opportunity since 2013** for DG net lease — if you underwrite the credit risk correctly. New construction with 15 years of term at 6.75% is a compelling yield in a world where Treasuries sit at 4.25%. The spread to risk-free is 250 bps. That is real compensation for real risk.

The danger zone: older vintage DG properties with 5-7 years remaining at 7.50%+ caps. These are priced for risk, and the risk is real. Rent resets, tenant non-renewal, and deferred maintenance can turn a "high yield" deal into a vacancy.
