# DG Lease Anatomy — The Standard Dollar General NNN Lease

**Source**: 10-K filings, CoStar, published net lease broker marketing, DG preferred developer disclosures
**Relevance**: This is the asset you're selling. Know every clause.

## The Standard New-Construction DG Lease

```
PRIMARY TERM:         15 years
LEASE TYPE:           Absolute NNN (Triple Net)
GUARANTOR:            Dollar General Corporation (NYSE: DG)
CREDIT RATING:        BBB (S&P) / Baa2 (Moody's) — Investment Grade
FRANCHISED:           NO — DG operates 100% corporate stores
RENT BUMPS:           10% every 5 years (compounding)
RENEWAL OPTIONS:      Six (6) 5-year options = 30 additional years
MAXIMUM TERM:         45 years (15 primary + 30 option years)
TYPICAL ANNUAL RENT:  $95,000 - $145,000 (varies by market)
```

## NNN Obligations — What the Tenant Pays

Under a true NNN Dollar General lease, the landlord's management burden is functionally zero:

| Expense Category | Paid By | Notes |
|-----------------|---------|-------|
| Base rent | Tenant (DG Corp) | Monthly, corporate wire |
| Property taxes | Tenant | 100% reimbursed or paid direct |
| Building insurance | Tenant | DG carries corporate master policy |
| Roof | Tenant | Repair AND replacement |
| Structure | Tenant | Foundation, walls, structural elements |
| HVAC | Tenant | All heating/cooling systems |
| Parking lot | Tenant | Resurfacing, striping, repair |
| CAM | Tenant | Landscaping, snow removal, signage |
| Interior repairs | Tenant | Flooring, fixtures, plumbing, electrical |
| Capital expenditures | Tenant | All building-related CapEx |

**Landlord responsibility: ZERO.** Collect rent. Pay mortgage. That's it.

This is why DG trades at sub-7 caps on new construction — the credit is investment-grade and the landlord has no management exposure.

## New Construction vs Existing — Cap Rate Comparison

| Attribute | New Construction | Existing Store |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| Lease term remaining | 15 years | 3-10 years typical |
| Lease type | Absolute NNN | NN or Modified NNN |
| Roof/structure | Tenant (NNN) | May revert to landlord |
| Cap rate range | 6.50% - 6.85% | 7.50% - 8.50%+ |
| Price (at $110K rent) | $1,607K - $1,692K | $1,294K - $1,467K |
| Buyer pool | REITs, 1031, DSTs, HNW | Value-add, local investors |
| Financing | 65-75% LTV, sub-6% rate | Tighter terms, higher rate |
| Rent bumps remaining | All 8 bumps intact | Fewer bumps remaining |
| Building condition | Brand new | Deferred maintenance risk |

**The spread**: New construction trades 75-175 bps tighter than existing. That spread IS the value of term. A 15-year corporate-guaranteed NNN lease from a BBB tenant is one of the safest income streams in commercial real estate.

## Why "Not Franchised" Matters

```
FRANCHISE MODEL (e.g., Subway, McDonald's):
  Lease guaranteed by: LOCAL OPERATOR (LLC with limited assets)
  Credit quality:      Unrated, often thin capitalization
  Default risk:        Operator bankruptcy ≠ brand bankruptcy
  Rent recovery:       Uncertain — must find replacement tenant

DG MODEL (100% corporate):
  Lease guaranteed by: DOLLAR GENERAL CORPORATION
  Credit quality:      BBB / Baa2 — investment grade
  Default risk:        $41.2B revenue company backs every lease
  Rent recovery:       Corporate guarantee survives individual store closure
  
  RESULT: Every DG lease carries the same credit.
  Store #1 in Goodlettsville, TN = Store #20,959 in rural Maine.
  Same guarantor. Same balance sheet. Same BBB rating.
```

This is the core value proposition for net lease investors: you're not underwriting a local operator — you're underwriting a $41 billion Fortune 50 company.

## Lease Risk Factors

```
WATCH FOR:
  1. Remaining term < 5 years    → Renewal risk, cap rate widens 100-200 bps
  2. NN vs NNN                   → Landlord retains roof/structure = real expense
  3. Below-market rent           → DG likely renews (good), but limited bump upside
  4. Above-market rent           → DG may not renew, relocation risk
  5. Store performance           → Dark stores exist — DG closed 96 in FY26
  6. Lease commencement date     → Older leases may have different bump structures
  7. Co-tenancy / exclusivity    → Some leases restrict competing tenants on pad
  8. Demolition clause           → Rare, but some leases allow DG to demolish at term end
```
