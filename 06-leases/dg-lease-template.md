# DG Lease Template — What to Look For

## Overview

Dollar General leases follow a standardized corporate template, but every deal has variations. This document breaks down the standard DG lease structure, NNN responsibility matrix, renewal mechanics, and red flags that can kill a deal or crater your valuation.

## Term Structure

### Primary Term: 15 Years

The standard DG lease is a 15-year primary term with six 5-year renewal options, totaling 45 years of potential occupancy.

```
STANDARD DG LEASE TIMELINE (15-YEAR PRIMARY + 6 OPTIONS)

Year 1-5:     BASE RENT ($85,000 - $100,000)
Year 6-10:    BASE + 10% ($93,500 - $110,000)
Year 11-15:   BASE + 20% ($102,000 - $120,000)
              ──────── END OF PRIMARY TERM ────────
Option 1:     5 years, +5-10% from Year 15 rent
Option 2:     5 years, +5-10% from Option 1 rent
Option 3:     5 years, +5-10% from Option 2 rent
Option 4:     5 years, +5-10% from Option 3 rent
Option 5:     5 years, +5-10% from Option 4 rent
Option 6:     5 years, +5-10% from Option 5 rent
              ──────── YEAR 45: LEASE EXPIRATION ────────
```

### Rent Escalation Math

On a $92,000 base rent with 10% bumps every 5 years during the primary term:

| Period | Annual Rent | Monthly Rent | Cumulative Paid |
|--------|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Year 1-5 | $92,000 | $7,667 | $460,000 |
| Year 6-10 | $101,200 (+10%) | $8,433 | $966,000 |
| Year 11-15 | $110,400 (+20% from base) | $9,200 | $1,518,000 |
| Option 1 (Yr 16-20) | $116,000 - $121,440 | $9,667 - $10,120 | ~$2,100,000 |
| Option 2 (Yr 21-25) | $121,800 - $133,584 | $10,150 - $11,132 | ~$2,700,000 |

**Key distinction**: Primary term bumps are typically calculated from BASE rent (Year 1-5 rent), not compounding. Option period bumps are typically calculated from the PRIOR option period rent (compounding). Read the lease carefully — this distinction matters for underwriting.

## NNN Responsibility Matrix

Dollar General leases are structured as **absolute NNN (triple net)** — tenant pays all operating expenses. This is the gold standard for passive income.

| Expense Category | Responsible Party | Notes |
|-----------------|-------------------|-------|
| Base rent | TENANT | Paid monthly, in advance |
| Property taxes | TENANT | Paid directly to taxing authority or reimbursed |
| Property insurance | TENANT | Tenant carries building + liability coverage |
| Roof | TENANT | Maintenance AND replacement |
| Structure | TENANT | Foundation, walls, structural components |
| HVAC | TENANT | Maintenance AND replacement |
| Parking lot | TENANT | Resurfacing, restriping, repair |
| CAM (common area) | TENANT | Snow removal, lighting, signage |
| Landscaping | TENANT | Mowing, irrigation, tree trimming |
| Plumbing | TENANT | Interior and exterior |
| Electrical | TENANT | Interior and exterior |
| Utilities | TENANT | Electric, gas, water, sewer, telecom |
| ADA compliance | TENANT | Interior and site accessibility |
| Environmental | TENANT | Typically tenant responsibility for operations |
| **Landlord obligation** | **NONE** | **True absolute NNN — landlord collects rent only** |

### What "Absolute NNN" Means in Practice

```
LANDLORD'S ANNUAL OBLIGATIONS ON A TRUE DG NNN LEASE:

1. Deposit rent check                    ($7,667/month)
2. File tax return on rental income      (once per year)
3. Nothing else.

No property management.
No maintenance calls.
No vendor coordination.
No capital reserves.
No insurance procurement.
```

## Renewal Mechanics

### Tenant Option Structure

- **Who holds the option**: TENANT (Dollar General) — not the landlord
- **Number of options**: Typically 6 options of 5 years each
- **Notice requirement**: Written notice 6-12 months prior to expiration (usually 6 months for DG)
- **Rent during renewal**: Fixed increase per option (5-10% above prior period)
- **Condition**: Tenant must not be in default at time of exercise

### What Happens at Expiration

If DG does NOT exercise a renewal option:

1. **Lease expires** — tenant vacates, returns premises in "broom clean" condition
2. **Landlord retains the building** — DG builds to suit but landlord owns the improvements
3. **Dark store risk** — 9,100 SF box in a rural market with limited re-tenancy options
4. **Re-leasing cost**: $15-30 PSF in TI + 6-18 months downtime (industry average for secondary retail)

### Critical: Options Are Tenant's Right, Not Obligation

DG is under no obligation to renew. Historical renewal rates vary:

| Remaining Primary Term | Renewal Probability | Market Impact |
|-----------------------|--------------------| --------------|
| 10+ years | N/A (not yet relevant) | Cap rates: 6.50-6.75% |
| 5-10 years | 80-85% estimated | Cap rates: 7.00-7.50% |
| 0-5 years | 65-75% estimated | Cap rates: 7.50-8.50% |
| In option period | 60-70% per option | Cap rates: 8.00-9.00%+ |

## Assignment and Sublease

### Standard DG Lease Provisions

- **Assignment by tenant**: Usually requires landlord consent, not to be unreasonably withheld
- **Assignment by landlord**: Landlord may freely assign (sell) the property; lease runs with the land
- **Sublease by tenant**: Usually permitted with landlord consent; DG remains primarily liable
- **Corporate merger/reorganization**: Typically excluded from assignment restrictions — DG can merge, restructure, or spin off without landlord approval

### What This Means for Investors

When you buy a DG property, the lease transfers automatically. No DG approval needed. No assignment fee. No estoppel negotiation in most cases (though always request one for diligence).

## Red Flags in Lease Review

These provisions can crater your deal or surprise you at resale. Check every lease for:

### Deal Killers

| Red Flag | What It Means | Impact |
|----------|--------------|--------|
| **Early termination clause** | Tenant can break the lease before expiration (usually tied to sales thresholds, condemnation, or casualty) | Destroys certainty of cash flow; cap rate expands 50-100+ bps |
| **Landlord maintenance obligations** | Landlord responsible for roof, structure, or HVAC | NOT true NNN — budget $2,000-5,000/yr for reserves; reprices the deal |
| **Percentage rent** | Landlord receives % of gross sales above a breakpoint | Signals older lease vintage; introduces variable income |
| **Co-tenancy requirement** | Tenant can reduce rent or terminate if anchor tenant leaves | Rare for DG standalone; common in strip center outparcels |

### Valuation Adjusters

| Red Flag | What It Means | Impact |
|----------|--------------|--------|
| **Exclusive use restriction** | Limits future use of property to specific retail categories | Constrains re-tenancy options if DG vacates |
| **ROFR (Right of First Refusal)** | Tenant has right to match any purchase offer | Slows the sales process; 30-60 day matching period |
| **Radius restriction** | Limits landlord from leasing to competing tenants within X miles | Only relevant for multi-tenant or portfolio owners |
| **Below-market bumps** | Escalations below 10% / 5 years | Underperforms inflation; cash flow erodes in real terms |
| **Personal guarantee vs corporate** | Lease guaranteed by individual (franchisee) vs DG corporate | DG is 100% corporate — if you see a personal guarantee, it is NOT a Dollar General lease |

### Verification Checklist

Before closing on any DG net lease acquisition:

```
LEASE DILIGENCE CHECKLIST:

[ ] Confirm CORPORATE guarantee (not individual/franchisee)
[ ] Verify remaining primary term (calculate to the month)
[ ] Confirm rent escalation schedule (base vs compounding)
[ ] Check NNN structure — ALL expenses tenant responsibility
[ ] Search for early termination provisions
[ ] Search for co-tenancy or kick-out clauses
[ ] Confirm renewal option terms and notice periods
[ ] Review assignment/sublease restrictions
[ ] Request estoppel certificate from DG
[ ] Confirm no percentage rent provisions
[ ] Verify landlord has NO maintenance obligations
[ ] Check for ROFR or ROFO provisions
[ ] Review environmental provisions and indemnification
[ ] Confirm insurance requirements and named insured
[ ] Review casualty/condemnation provisions (who gets proceeds?)
```

## Standard vs Non-Standard Leases

Not all DG leases are created equal. Vintage matters.

| Lease Era | Typical Structure | Watch For |
|-----------|------------------|-----------|
| Pre-2005 | Modified gross or NN | Landlord roof/structure obligations |
| 2005-2012 | NNN, 15yr, 8-10% bumps | Smaller bumps, shorter options |
| 2012-2018 | Absolute NNN, 15yr, 10% bumps | Standard template, clean |
| 2018-present | Absolute NNN, 15yr, 10% bumps | Cleanest version, most investor-friendly |
| Sale-leaseback | Varies by negotiation | Custom terms, verify every clause |

## Broker Takeaway

The DG lease is one of the cleanest NNN documents in the market — but "clean" does not mean "identical." Every lease has its own escalation schedule, option terms, and buried provisions. The difference between a 6.75% cap deal and a 7.50% cap deal is often one clause: an early termination right, a landlord roof obligation, or a missing rent bump. Read the lease. Every page. Every exhibit. The economics are in the details.
